# 🎤 Presentation Guidelines for Large Audience (Big Screen)

## 🎯 Goal
Create a visually stunning, high-impact presentation designed for a large audience and big screens. The content must be easy to understand at a glance and emotionally engaging.

---

## 🧠 Core Principles

- **Clarity over quantity**  
  Avoid too much text. Each slide should communicate ONE main idea.

- **Visual-first approach**  
  Prioritize visuals over text: images, icons, charts, diagrams.

- **Instant readability**  
  The audience should understand the slide in **3–5 seconds**.

- **Impactful storytelling**  
  Slides should feel like a story, not a document.

---

## 🪄 Design Guidelines

### 🧩 Layout
- Use **large margins** and lots of whitespace
- Keep layouts simple and balanced
- Avoid clutter at all costs

### 🔤 Typography
- Use **large font sizes** (minimum ~28–32pt)
- Prefer short phrases over paragraphs
- Highlight key words using bold or color

### 🎨 Colors
- Use a **clean, consistent color palette**
- Ensure strong contrast (especially for big screens)
- Use color to guide attention (not decoration)

---

## 🖼️ Visual Elements

- Use **high-quality images** (no pixelation on big screens)
- Prefer **icons and illustrations** instead of text
- Use **charts/graphs** instead of raw data
- Replace bullet points with **visual layouts** when possible

---

## ⚡ Slide Content Rules

- Max **5–7 words per line**
- Max **2–3 lines per slide**
- Avoid paragraphs
- One idea per slide

---

## 🎬 Animations & Transitions

- Use **subtle animations** to guide attention
- Avoid overusing effects
- Animations should support storytelling, not distract

---

## 🧭 Flow & Storytelling

- Start with a **strong hook**
- Build a clear narrative:
  - Problem → Insight → Solution → Impact
- End with a **memorable closing**

---

## 🔥 Impact Techniques

- Use **big numbers** (highlight key metrics)
- Use **before/after comparisons**
- Use **contrast** (bad vs good, old vs new)
- Include **real-world examples or visuals**

---

## 🚫 Avoid

- Walls of text
- Small fonts
- Complex tables
- Too many elements on one slide
- Reading directly from slides

---

## ✅ Success Criteria

A slide is successful if:
- It can be understood in seconds
- It looks clean and modern
- It supports the speaker, not replaces them
- It creates a visual impact

---

## 💡 Extra Suggestions

- Think: **"Would this look good on a huge screen?"**
- Design for people sitting far away
- Use emotion: surprise, curiosity, clarity
- When in doubt → simplify

---

## 🧠 Mental Model

> "Slides are not documents. They are visual support for a story."
